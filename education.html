1. DESIGN HEALTHCARE
FOR ALL BODIES

For centuries, medicine built its foundations on a dangerous assumption: that the male body could represent all of humanity. The result? A healthcare system that systematically excludes half the population, leading to delayed diagnoses, inappropriate treatments, and preventable deaths.

48%
of female deaths are from cardiovascular disease vs 38% male deaths
90%
of drug studies in 2007 were conducted exclusively on male subjects
1.7x
more likely for women to experience drug side effects than men

"At first, I noticed that I couldn't walk uphill without feeling out of breath. My doctor suspected asthma, but all tests came back negative. One day, I felt so unwell that I ended up in the emergency room... Two days later, I had a heart attack."

Eve Walker, 28, Heart Attack Survivor

2. LISTEN TO THE CYCLE
THAT DEFINES US

For decades, menstrual product testing used artificial blood substitutes, leaving gaps in understanding how products perform with real menstrual flow. Meanwhile, the menstrual cycle—a vital health indicator—has been dismissed as inconvenient rather than recognized as a window into overall wellbeing.

1/3
of menstruating individuals experience heavy menstrual bleeding
2022
First time real blood was used in menstrual product testing
24%
difference between highest and lowest tampon tax rates in Europe

3. TEST FOR EVERYONE
NOT JUST THE DEFAULT

After more than 50 years of crash testing, the first female dummy was created in 2022. Until then, cars were "safety tested" using only male dummies, sometimes simply downsized without considering real biological differences. The result? Cars designed to protect men, not women.

17%
higher probability for women to die in a car accident
73%
higher probability of sustaining serious injuries in head-on collisions

"I don't have the male pelvis that the seat belt was designed for [...] Instead of hitting the bone, it slid up and hit my stomach, and that's how it burst my intestine."

Maria Weston Kuhn, survivor of a 2019 car crash

4. PROTECT ALL WORKERS,
NOT HALF OF THEM

Women represent a growing percentage of workers in physically demanding fields like military, firefighting, and construction. Yet the protective equipment they depend on is designed with men in mind, creating safety risks and barriers to performance that could be easily avoided.

16%
of active-duty military personnel are women
8%
of firefighters are women
>10%
of construction workers are women

"From my very first day on the job, I noticed that the safety gear didn’t fit me properly. The gloves in particular were a constant. They only came in men’s sizes, and even the smallest pair was far too big for my hands. The fingers were loose, the fabric bunched up, and I could never get a proper grip. Still, I had no choice but to wear them."

Anya Keefe, 35 years old
