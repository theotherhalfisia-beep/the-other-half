1. DESIGN HEALTHCARE
FOR ALL BODIES

For centuries, medicine built its foundations on a dangerous assumption: that the male body could represent all of humanity. The result? A healthcare system that systematically excludes half the population, leading to delayed diagnoses, inappropriate treatments, and preventable deaths.

2. LISTEN TO THE CYCLE

Meanwhile, the menstrual cycle—a vital health indicator—has been dismissed as inconvenient rather than recognized as a window into overall wellbeing.

4. PROTECT ALL WORKERS, NOT HALF OF THEM

Women represent a growing percentage of workers in physically demanding fields like military, firefighting, and construction. Yet the protective equipment they depend on is designed with men in mind, creating safety risks and barriers to performance that could be easily avoided.

16%
of active-duty military personnel are women
8%
of firefighters are women
>10%
of construction workers are women

"From my very first dayon the job, I noticed that the safety gear didn’t fit me properly. The gloves in particular were a constant. They only came in men’s sizes, and even the smallest pair was far too big for my hands. The fingers were loose, the fabric bunched up, and I could never get a proper grip. Still, I had no choice but to wear them.

Anya Keefe, 35 years old.
